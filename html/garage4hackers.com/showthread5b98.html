    Be Careful - LONG noob post ahead :)

    Hey guys, I am new here and this look like a neat and clean place to learn!

    For starters, I would like to consult with the experienced lot of you

    I am 24 years old. Until the age of 18 I didn't take the hacking\security world too seriously, and it all boiled down to hacking in very simple ways (WIN NT password cracking, NetBios Hacking etc. - If you can even call it hacking, now that I was exposed to some of the real deal on forums and on videos from Defcon and Blackhat I get a bit sad because I am LIGHT YEARS behind those brilliant minds) to start FTP servers and some other stuff. Used to scan some ports and see if I can do something with it (at a VERY superficial level, as you can imagine). Did some social engineering for the dark side, mixed with some pinches and trojans experiences (regret it 'till this day, please don't flame me. I am not a bad person), however the experience did me good, I am sure.

    I decided a while back that I want to do this as a real thing and have a career. I started a 1-year course to get certified to the following:
    Microsoft MCITP 2008 Server Administrator (passed 2/3 tests so far)
    Checkpoint CCSA (about to finish this module - Going to apply to the exam pretty soon after I finish the MS part)
    Checkpoint CCSE
    COMPTIA Linux +
    Cisco CCNA

    After that I plan to find a job in this field (hoping someone would hire a guy like me. An enthusiast who likes to learn and learn fast, but with no solid experience). And while working get certified for CEH and after that perhaps go for the EC-Council Certified Security Analyst and the LPT (Licensed Penetration Tester) because the offensive part seems more attractive and appealing to me. HOWEVER - My tutor advised me to look into what Offensive Security has to offer, and perhaps set OSCP as a future goal somewhere in between.

    As I watch videos from the latest Defcon (19th) and Blackhat, I am simply amazed at how these guys got to those levels of knowledge. I guess they just did it brick by brick. And FYI, this is the only educational path I am taking right now, e.g I am not also on the academy taking Computer Science or anything...

    And so I came to the conclusion that courses and certifications can only give you so much. So I have set a few goals for the next month, to finish these 2 books, in hope they would give me a fundamental entry level to this world "hacker-wise":

    Hacking The Art of Exploitation 2nd Edition
    Morgan Kaufmann - The Illustrated Network How TCPIP Works in a Modern Network

    Also planning to learn some PHP (I have a very basic knowledge of C# and Javascript).

    I guess the end goal is to reach a CISSP by the age of, I don't know, 30? 35? 40?

    I want to do good in this field and I want to earn money because I plan to have a family sometime and I want them to have the best, because we, people, deserve the best (most of us, I guess - That's just the way I see it).

    Please, go easy on me as I am new not only in this forum, but officially in this Security world.

    Thanks for those who read this all the way through.
