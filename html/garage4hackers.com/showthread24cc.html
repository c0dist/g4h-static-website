    DEFCON 19 - Network Forensics Contest

    I decided to take some time and do a complete write up on how I went about solving the DEFCON 19 Network Forensics Puzzle.

    I'll share my method here and if you guys have an alternate workaround, you can post it here in this thread.

    I am also planning on preparing a video where I demonstrate the complete procedure and it would be helpful for those who prefer a visual writeup instead.

    Scenario: The lead chemist of a high-profile pharmaceutical company was involved in a serious accident, leaving him in a coma days before the release of the company’s highly publicized “133t pill.” The chemist was the only person in possession of the list of ingredients required to produce the wonder drug, and it is not known if he will ever recover. All chemical evidence of the drug has been destroyed, but the company believes that the missing ingredients may have been stored electronically. You have been hired as a forensic investigator, to recover the final ingredient of their 133t pill. Can you find the missing ingredient?

    The Network Forensics Puzzle this time, consisted of 6 rounds. These were in the form of TrueCrypt Volumes, password protected. When you solve one Puzzle, you will be provided the password for the next Round's TrueCrypt Volume.

    You can download the main file (consisting of all the rounds) here: http://forensicscontest.com/contest0...011-Contest.tc

    The password for this TrueCrypt volume is: !#$h1d3&&s33k$#!

    Once you have mounted it. You will find inside seperate folders for each Round.

    Password for Round# 1: r0und1g0!!

    Ok. So, once you have mounted this volume. You will find inside:

    • An HTML file
    • A wireshark network capture file (.pcap format).


    You have to analyze the network capture file to find out the answers to questions given in the HTML File.

    For the sake of simplicity, I will mention the Questions and Answers both in this thread. If you want to try it out yourself before reading this content. You can skip the other posts in this thread. Once you have completed solving the Puzzle Round, you can revisit this thread.
