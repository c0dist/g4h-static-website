    Yes, you know the terms, just need to google more about them.
    Generally, 3rd parties are hired to perform required Penetration Testing. Following could be their different forms:

    1. Network Penetration Test - mostly covering Servers & Network devices (ports & services)
    - further, it could be Internal penetration test or external penetration test

    2. Web Application Penetration Test - mostly covering vulnerabilities on Web applications & services (port 80, port 443)
    - again, based on requirement, it could be covering internet facing web applications of organization or internal apps not accessible from Internet

    3. Wireless Penetration Test - To check security of Access points etc.

    4. All of above mentioned could also be categorised under "Black Box Penetration Testing" (though rarely you get opportunity for that but it's the most difficult and exciting), White Box Penetration Testing, Gray Box Penetration Testing

    ... anything that could process data, store data, provide some service could be part of some kind of penetration testing.

    Few more terms for you to google - "Penetration Test" & "Vulnerability Assessment"
