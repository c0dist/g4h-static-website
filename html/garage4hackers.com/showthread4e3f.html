    I have huge amount of energy for studying. Like I recently started studying Assembly programming, within 20 days i gripped it well & was able to make a tutorial thread here.

    Hey Mark. Well, this is how I decided I should be in the future . When i was in college, I am a gaming addict (DotA mainly).I was the Captain of a popular clan in GG which naturally means i didn't cover the college subjects well. There are three reasons for that, gaming, no proper teachers, but the main reason is that I always asked myself "WTF am i gonna do with that?". Looking back I realize what a great mistake I have done. Judging by the videos you made, i assume that you are more inclined towards exploit development. So for that, you need strong OS skills, not just the theory but also the code. Try reading about the Linux kernel(there are a plenty of books out there, the one I started just now is Linux kernel architecture). Then without compilers knowledge, you cant be an "expert" reverse engineer (note expert). So read about it too. There is a compilers course @ http://coursera.org. It is starting in 2 weeks i guess. I am gonna enroll. Moving from user space exploitation to kernel space exploitation requires you to have knowledge on device driver development, knowing the internals of OS etc, learn about user mode & kernel mode debugging. Only book that is highly useful for reversing is Reversing:secrets of reverse engineers. Know proper Data structures and algorithms , it is a must for whatever big project you are working on. Being an expert in all these will take like 2-3 years I guess. These are all the things I wanted to do in my path so I am sharing you these. Also, when you are free audit some open source code. Remember, all the knowledge you acquire will be of no use if you dont put them to use. And the rest,my friend, as they say is history.

    Best Regards.
