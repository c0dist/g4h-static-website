    Hi Hackinglife. This is the same restless stage I have gone through. After preparing for a target course, I would strongly advice you to go for OSCP. This is the course that will set you apart from others. But before that you need to meet some basic requirements so that you don't suffer in middle of the course. Get good command in Linux, learn Python (it will help you during final test for creating a plan for auto reconnaissance and save your time alot), get/study CCNA, go through CEH cbt nuggets study material (by james conrad i guess, learn the basic fundamentals), must learn Assembly language. It could take approx. 1 yr. Then you should be ready to for PWB/OSCP. Don't expect for a good paying job initially. You may have to work without payment also in order to gain experience. I have seen people working on short term contracts basis with XXX company without getting paid (just for experience). After getting OSCP you will yourself get to know what to do next. Good Luck

    let me try to answer your questions..
    1. For good command over Linux, you can either take the RHCE course, but if you're not interested in certification, I'd suggest you to learn from online tuts.. try to get command over scripting, various servers, bit of internal working of OS, etc.
    2. Why python? Cause python is the language of choice for scripting by many people. python allows you to write scripts in a really short time due to extensive libs. and most of the tools out there are either written in python, Perl or ruby. Learning python will help you in understanding as well as writing codes really fast.
    3. Though I've no idea about what is the syllabus of CCNA, but if possible I'll advise you to take proper knowledge of networks, and networking devices like routers, etc. And seems like you might have idea of it already.

    I myself am a beginner and a learner. Hope this helps. And everyone, please correct me if I'm wrong at any point..

    I guess Python part & Linux is cleared by Rahul. Do you know?- after just establishing a session with any webserver, only few Python instruction can reveal its OS:-
    > import urllib
    >import sys
    >wpage=urllib.urlopen('http://abcd.com')
    >print wpage.info()

    Isn't that awesome? You get the info of the server running the webpage.

    C/C++:- A primitive still powerful language, still used by hackers to extract out the information out of the servers/domain controllers, but requires stronger coding skills & practice, Python is better.

    Cursera.org/programming1 :- I havn't gone through through the course, but you should know good programming basics in order to understand the structures used,objects/class/modules/inheritance for reverse engineering.

    CCNA:- Get CBT nuggets for CCNA, a must to understand subnetting, routing, Vlans, routing protocols, networking basics. Don't skip it.

    Linux:- Get CBTs for RHCE (a beautiful one by Dean Davis), not required to cover all of the topics, but AFAIK it is the best source for mastering Linux.

    Assembly:- You would find hell lot of videos on youtube. One I recommend :- Assembly primer by Vivek Ramachandran on "securitytube.net", and an E-book "assembly programming from groundup". I learnt from these only.

    Remember, Assembly is most helpful for creating your own exploits and creating codecaves. Further, there are OSCP holders also in this forum, "over and out" to them.

    You can get most of these tutorials through torrent. And also try to get SANS 560 GPEN course material. A nice one.
    This should be huge enough. Good luck...
