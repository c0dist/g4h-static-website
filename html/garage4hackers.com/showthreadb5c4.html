    The most important point to be noted for Jobs abroad is 'Visa Requirements'

    And this is varying for each country or region.

    1)Middle East-Moderately Easy
    2)Europe-Very Tough
    3)N.America-Very Tough
    4)Singapore/Malaysia-Moderately Easy
    5)Africa-Easy
    6)Australia-Tough(b0nd your inputs)

    There are too many visa types which you will have to know.

    Few points from my experience,
    1)Job opportunities are too many in Europe & N.America in Security Industry
    2) You will get interview calls very quickly.
    3)The first question in a interview will be whether you hold a Tier-II or Tier-I visa.
    4)If yes then you are sure you can land a job.
    5)In no they wont take one single step ahead

    One critical point we have to note is Western Countries have brought in serious Curbs in immigration caps,which have put Indians moving abroad.

    The easy way to move abroad right now is join any MNC in India,use their Inter Company Transfer Visa route to get there and then lookout.

    But tht doesnt mean there is any exceptions,some companies go out of their way and defy government regulations and get ppl.

    Few tips to improve your likelihood of getting Jobs abroad.

    1) Have an excellent CV with your achievements and goals.
    2)Certifications matter for few regions especially Middle East.
    3)Most importantly network.thts the only way to get jobs abroad....build contacts.

    Hi Amol,

    That's a good query raised by you. Most of the people simply don't apply and loose the opportunity.

    AnArKI has already stated quite strong points and I would like to add few more based on my experience:

    1. Singapore and Australia have very good market for InfoSec. But Singapore InfoSec expects same as what our local Industry does - they want you to know Pentesting and Process both and at good level. CISSP is a big certificate and kinda must there. Salary could be good but not too attractive until you have 6-8 years of experience and are holding a good position. The best part is that visa is bit easy to get and it's a jumping pad to get into other foreign countries. Tax is quite less and distance from native country is just 5.30 hours of direct flight.

    2. Australia is booming. The best part I like about it is the demand of technical skills. Many of them are hunting for core technical skills and accordingly they pay quite good. Taxes are quite high but accordingly are the salaries. The core technical companies value your skills and expects you to explore more and present the organization in conferences.
    Now comes the visa part and that's really not easy to get through.
    Definitely no organization wants to get into visa formalities, visa and relocation expenses etc. Moreover they prefer face-to-face interviews and a bit scared of picking you up from where you are currently residing. Your credibility gets bonus points if you are already outside and in countries like Singapore or New Zealand or European countries, US etc.
    Furthermore, big companies are "accredited employers" and they have permission from government to hire foreigners. But small companies don't. They must prove first that they couldn't get local talent and therefore need to hire a foreigner. Big companies do audit work as well and would need you to know both, pentesting and process.

    The ways you could secure a job outside (AUS, NZ):
    1. Good talent. But you should have proof of that - your experience, your research papers, your exploits, tools, scripts, conferences etc. speaks for you. And they do respect them! If you have got these in your CV, there are high chances of getting calls from those small but core technical companies. Without those skills in you, they would have tough time justifying your hiring.
    2. Professional contacts so that someone could recommend you.

    So it boils down to your homework - how strong your skills are and how strong your presence is on internet.....

    At the same time, while consolidating your skills, keep trying for relevant jobs on seek job portal for AUS/NZ. You never know an opportunity might be waiting for you. And it wouldn't be bad if you receive just 1 call in couple of months.
