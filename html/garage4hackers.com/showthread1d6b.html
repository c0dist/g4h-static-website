    Just a few thoughts about rwthCTF ..

    First of all I was floored by the competition ..very very challenging and the team that won it deserves it. Also, The organizing team put up a nice show.

    I was not knowing this CTF was up in league with likes of DefconCTF. Anyways, it was classic CTF and not the web based CTF. The thing with classic CTF is that it is different ball game all together. You have to defend as well as attack at the same time. This makes life hard.

    What they did was gave us was vulnerable image of Linux box and we had to ensure it remains reachable from their scoreboard setup monitoring server. The different teams taking part would be scoring by attacking the services in our vulnerable box and putting them down. Downtime of services meant we lost points.
    Hence each team had to defend their box as well as attack on other teams vulnerable box at the same time.It was mayhem.

    The services( total 5) on the vulnerable box included a web php forum , a binary program with C source code, a editor program, etc. All were listening at different ports and the main game monitoring server was refreshing them with different flags at different time. This means the flag stole by attacking teams was valid only for a while.

    The other things in CTF was that they had set up a market place where you could trade exploits and hacks with other teams.

    But the main part of CTF was to solve the 5 challenges they gave you ( 1st was a problem based on DNA cryptography I think ) .Clearing the 5th challenge would give you access to a real robot they had put up in a room. You would be given live streaming of that room. You would have to control the real robot remotely and have to maneuver it to press a switch, all while viewing the web cam feed they had for you of the robot.

    All in all, the rwthCTF was the first time I tasted an international CTF. First time I saw a hacking competition which ensured that team work was more important for winning, rather than having one man show. Expert sync between team members communication was more necessary.

    I am awed by the skills of people who organized it. All that VPN and server setup they did and the challenges and programs they wrote especially for the CTF. Just the scoreboard they had put up was such a fabulous piece of programming.It was pinging all the teams every 5 secs and displaying live results ! How can these guys be so light years ahead



    Anyways, I think I would like to participate next year too.
    Means of preparation would be perhaps playing things like Wargames,STS etc online. Learning ninja exploitation stuff. Plus everything else
