    Anant Shrivastava
    There are multiple things at stake here. you first need to take a step back and analyze the situation and also answer some of the questions.

    1) this is your first job.
    2) based on your question, i suppose potential pay is not a question.
    3) One company appears to be consulting firm. ideally that means, onsite opportunities, visitng different places. shorter project timeframes. also most of your work should ideally be targetting offensive work. (attack exploit and profit)
    4) Second company appears to be a product firm and your role seems to be more of the area dev/sec/ops. You not only attack or find flaws but you will keep working with same team trying to solve the challenges.

    with this in mind. try to ask some questions to yourself.

    1) What is it that you like in infosec. (saying everything is good will not help. do you like offense or defence.)
    2) Do you want to keep working with a single team and keep upgrading the level. (if they co-operate) or work on your way to get things sorted. Or on the other hand work with a team for max of a month generally a week or two and move on. may be return back to them once a six month or a year.
    3) option 2 is a research area, there might be more failures then sucess with option 2 are you comfortable with that. Option 1 will generally give you a weekend satisfaction that you compromised something or the other (generally its easy to find flaws if you are looking at different things every week).

    Ultimately it will depend on you. everyone will push their own idea's but ultimately its you who has to work and endure the environment.


    Also remember this is not a make or break decision as its your first job. Finality of your career choices generally come around year 3-4 after which it gets hard to switch roles / streams of work except moving management.

    And last but not the least don't ever select a job coz of the big package. initial 2-4 years what matters more is how much you learn and how much you accept that in your daily life.
